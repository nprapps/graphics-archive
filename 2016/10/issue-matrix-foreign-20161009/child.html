Notes

1. At first, Clinton supported the TPP, but she now says the final product doesn’t meet her standards.
2. Johnson at one time expressed skepticism of the TPP but now supports it because he says it advances free trade.
3. Clinton has said she would impose “targeted tariffs” on companies that “break the rules.”
4. Stein wants to replace “corporate trade agreements with fair trade agreements.”
5. Johnson has said he has “mixed feelings.”
6. Trump has said he will remain “neutral” in order to help Israel and Palestine make a deal.
7. In a video posted to YouTube in 2012, Johnson said the U.S. should remain Israel’s ally but shouldn’t dictate actions when it comes to establishing a Palestinian statehood.
8. Stein supports “bringing all parties together as equals to seek workable solutions.”
9. In a July 31 interview, Trump said “the people of Crimea, from what I’ve heard, would rather be with Russia than where they were.”
10. Stein told Vox that the U.S. should move forward with diplomacy.
11. Clinton has indicated that she “will take on foreign countries that keep their goods artificially cheap by manipulating their currencies” but hasn’t explicitly said she will label China as a currency manipulator.
12. Stein has not said anything specifically on this issue.
13. Trump has previously said the U.S. won’t remain in NATO if other countries don’t “put up more money.” He has since changed his policy.
14. Stein told Vox: “I think we need to be clear about NATO’s role, especially in creating a more dangerous world right now.”
15. Trump does not believe in man-made climate change.
16. Johnson believes the free market will stop coal production and turn to different means of fuel.
17. Stein has said the agreement is not enough to curb climate change.
18. Trump has said “it’s going to happen anyway” on the question of nuclear proliferation and has said he would rather Japan have them than North Korea.
19. Johnson hasn’t said he is okay with more countries having nuclear weapons, but he is skeptical of the U.S. intervening in the affairs of other countries.
20. Stein wants global nuclear disarmament.
