Injury Rates Higher Than Average At Justice’s Delinquent Mines

There have been 147 injuries at Justice’s delinquent mines in delinquent years. These mines’ injury rates — nonfatal days lost per 200,000 employee-hours worked — exceeded the national average.

Notes

— Facilities process mined coal.
— Injury rates were calculated by taking raw Department of Labor enforcement data for the delinquent Justice mines and applying MSHA’s prescribed formulas for Nonfatal Days Lost Injuries (injuries that force time away from work or a change in assignment) that occurred while those mines were delinquent.

Source: NPR analysis of Department of Labor data
