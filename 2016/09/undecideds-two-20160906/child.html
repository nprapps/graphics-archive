A Big Share Of Voters Are Choosing Someone Other Than Clinton Or Trump (Or Just Haven’t Chosen)

When pollsters ask them if they would choose Clinton or Trump, a remarkably high share of voters are choosing neither, saying they’d vote for someone else or are undecided — right now, it’s around 14 percent. If this year were anything like 2012 (Obama vs. Romney) or 2008 (Obama vs. McCain), that would fall off soon … but then, this election is pretty unusual.

Notes

Methodology: All polls are of likely or registered voters. All dates reflect polls’ end dates. For any date on which multiple polls ended, we averaged the results together. Each line represents a 10-polling-date moving average.
