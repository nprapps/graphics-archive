New For 2016: Voting Law Changes Since The 2012 Election

Notes

— Pending: These states have enacted new laws relating to voter identification or registration, but the laws have not yet gone into effect and may not apply to elections in 2016.
— Voter ID laws: Tennessee enacted a strict voter photo ID requirement in 2010. In 2013, it excluded county and municipal IDs as acceptable forms of identification. The National Conference of State Legislatures notes that there is some debate as to whether Alabama’s photo ID requirement can be considered “strict”: If two election officials sign sworn statements that they know the voter, a person can avoid having to fill out a provisional ballot and return later to show identification. Virginia moved from a strict non-photo ID requirement (implemented in 2012) to a strict photo ID requirement in 2014.
— Voter registration laws: In Missouri, a voter can register online using a mobile or touchscreen device. Desktop computer users must print out and mail a form.

Source: National Conference of State Legislatures, Brennan Center for Justice
