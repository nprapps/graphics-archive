Presidential Candidates Usually Go For Geographic Diversity

The conventional wisdom is that presidential nominees pick running mates from other regions to expand their appeal. And when they don’t, the thinking goes, they usually have a specific reason (like Arkansas’ Bill Clinton picking Tennessee’s Al Gore in 1992 to boost Democrats in the South). Below, candidates who chose someone from their same region are not bolded.
