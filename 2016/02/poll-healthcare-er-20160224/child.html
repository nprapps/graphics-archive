Why The ER?

A third of respondents nationwide said that they had received health care in the emergency room of a hospital in the past two years. Of those:

Purpose of most recent ER visit

Treatment for a major health problem (like a broken bone, cut or high fever) 40%
Some other reason 36%
Treatment for a minor health problem (like a sprain or toothache) 23%

Source: NPR/Robert Wood Johnson Foundation/Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health poll “Patients’ Perspectives on Health Care in the United States.” Interviews were conducted via telephone (landline and cellphone) in English and Spanish Sept. 8-Nov. 9, 2015. For national results, the margin of error is +/- 3.8 percentage points.

Credit: Alyson Hurt/NPR

Reason for choosing the ER

Other facilities were not open or patient could not get an appointment 28%
Brought to the emergency room by an ambulance 18%
Felt the emergency room was the only place that would treat them 16%
Some other reason 12%
Other facilities did not have the necessary staff or equipment 11%
Might need to be admitted to the hospital overnight 11%
Other facilities were too far away 3%
