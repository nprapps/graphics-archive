How Often Do Supreme Court Justices Speak?

Justice Thomas recently asked his first question in more than a decade. This puts him far from his peers, according to SCOTUSblog, which has data on the last five Supreme Court October terms (that is, prior to this current one), For the last five of those terms, he has averaged zero statements per argument, while his colleagues have averaged 7.7 or more. (SCOTUSblog counts all of these as “questions.” See footnote for an explanation of how they count.)

Notes

SCOTUSblog tallies up the number of times a justice’s name shows up in the transcript in all-capital letters. Because the chief justice makes what SCOTUSblog calls “administrative comments,” the blog subtracts three statements from his total during any given argument.
