How The Iran Vote Is Different

Congress’ vote on the Iran deal is similar to how it has previously handled debt ceiling increases: The vote is structured as a vote to disapprove. More members of Congress are able to express their disapproval, and there is a higher bar for blocking major actions.

Current Balance Of Power In Congress

Most Bills In Congress Involve Votes To Approve.

1. Congress votes on the bill.

Minimum votes needed to pass a bill: 218 in the House and 51 in the Senate (or 60, to end a filibuster)

With The Iran Deal, Congress Will Vote To Disapprove.

With this approach, proponents of the deal are counting on Congress not having enough votes to override a presidential veto. Meanwhile, opponents have to meet a higher bar to prevent the deal. (92% of the House and 98% of the Senate voted to handle it this way.)
