St. Bernard Parish Today Is Smaller and More Diverse

St. Bernard Parish experienced some of the most dramatic population losses in the New Orleans metropolitan area. Today the parish has returned to two-thirds of its 2000 population. Growth has slowed almost to the pre-storm level of 1 percent per year. However, unlike the rest of the metroplitan area, the new population is dramatically more racially diverse than the old one. In the charts below, the blue line shows that only half of the white population has returned. In contrast, the African-American population, shown by the green line, quickly resumed rapid growth that began before the storm and has now nearly doubled.
