Minnesota

Minnesota has the dubious distinction of being home to the most Syria travelers. They are all local Somali-American men. Sixteen Somali-Americans have been accused publicly of having joined ISIS or al-Qaida's arm in Syria and Iraq. U.S. law enforcement officials tell NPR the actual number of Minnesotans who have traveled, are thinking of traveling or have made it to Syria is more than 40. Part of the reason? Young men who went to Somalia nearly a decade ago to fight for al-Shabab, al-Qaida's arm there, have told friends and relatives in the Twin Cities to join ISIS instead.

ISIS has told its followers to attack military and police targets at home if they won't travel to Syria to join the group.
