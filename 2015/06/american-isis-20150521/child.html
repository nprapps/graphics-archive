In each case, a young fighter promised the young women marriage and a new life in a Muslim homeland.

Minnesota

In Minnesota, authorities have unsealed nine indictments against local Somali-American men. Sixteen Somali-Americans have been accused publicly of having joined ISIS or al-Qaida's arm in Syria and Iraq. U.S. law enforcement officials tell NPR the actual number of Minnesotans who have traveled, are thinking of traveling, or have made it to Syria, is more than 40. Part of the reason? Young men who went to Somalia nearly a decade ago to fight for al-Shabab, al-Qaida's arm there, have told friends and relatives in the Twin Cities to join ISIS instead.
