Scenario 1:
Same-sex marriage legal nationwide

The Court overturns all state bans.

Scenario 2:
States may ban same-sex marriage

If the Court upholds bans on same-sex marriage, state bans would be legal, but not all states may choose to impose them. In states that did impose bans, state courts would also need to decide what that would mean for couples that have already been married.

 

Scenario 3:
Interstate recognition is required

The Court upholds state bans on performing same-sex marriage, but requires states to recognize marriages legally performed in other states.

The map in this case is identical to Scenario 2, but in addition every state would be required to recognize same-sex marriages performed in states where they are legal.

Notes

1 Includes states with stayed court cases overturning their bans.
2 In Alabama, a federal judge struck down the state's same-sex marriage ban, but stayed her own decision pending the Supreme Court's decision.
3 In Missouri, a same-sex marriage ban is in effect, but marriage licenses are still being issued in some places.
4 In Kansas, there is not a same-sex marriage ban in effect, but not all counties are issuing marriage licenses to same-sex couples.
5 "State action" includes laws, ballot measures and court cases that legalize same-sex marriage at the state level.
