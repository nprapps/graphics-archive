Notes

— Alabama does not have an agency to monitor dams. The National Inventory of Dams, maintained by the Army Corps of Engineers, lists 2,241 dams in Alabama.
— Dams are designated “high-hazard potential” when their collapse could cost human life. The classification is not related to their actual integrity.
— Data only include state-regulated dams, and do not include those that are federally-regulated.
— In some cases, a “high-hazard potential” dam might be inspected more than once in a year.
— The mandated inspection frequency for high-hazard potential dams varies from state to state and ranges from every one to six years. The Model State Dam Safety Program, published by FEMA, recommends annual inspection of high-hazard potential dams.
