Inside The Telescope

back next

Starlight enters the telescope. First it hits the nearly 28-foot-wide, doughnut-shaped primary mirror. The light is reflected toward the secondary mirror, which is mounted about 20 feet away.

The secondary mirror reflects the light down to a dish-shaped tertiary mirror, which is carved out of the same piece of glass as the primary mirror.

The tertiary mirror then focuses the light into a camera that captures an image of the sky. This telescope design is relatively compact. Unlike other telescopes, it allows astronomers to look at a fairly big chunk of sky in one image.
