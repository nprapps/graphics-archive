Judicial Vacancy Rate, By U.S. Senate Delegation

Under President Obama, 88 percent of judicial vacancies are in states with one or more Republican senators. At this point in President George W. Bush’s administration, 57 percent of judicial vacancies were in states with one or more Democratic senators.

Notes

1. “No senators” includes places that do not have representation in the Senate (such as Washington, D.C., and Puerto Rico), and the Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit.

2. These data include both vacancies in place and future vacancies that have already been announced. They do not include vacancies created or announced within the past six months.

Source: Data analysis by Russell Wheeler/Brookings Institution
